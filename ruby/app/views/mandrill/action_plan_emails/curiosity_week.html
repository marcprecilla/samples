What's Curiosity Week?
Remember how curious you were as a child? Would you say that you are still as intrinsically interested and curious with your surroundings today? As we get older and take on more responsibilities in life, we naturally lose a bit of our curiosity and interest in the world around us. This is quite unfortunate, as positive psychology research has demonstrated that curiosity has a wide array of cognitive and social benefits. For one, curiosity is associated with better problem-solving skills – think about the curious mad scientist stereotype that is interested in figuring out how everything works, leading him to breakthrough innovations. Curiosity also benefits our social relationships – curious people tend to be considered good listeners, and often bring their own rich array of experiences into conversations.

Last but not least, curiosity has been linked to performance at school and at work. When we develop an insatiable thirst for knowledge and information, there are no limits as to what we can learn or achieve.

Let’s give our curiosity a boost this week!
